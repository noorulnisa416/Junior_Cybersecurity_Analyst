## 📖 Overview

This module lays the foundation for the entire course. It introduces what cybersecurity actually means, why personal and organizational data is valuable, who wants to steal it, and the different types of attackers behind cybercrime. 🌐🔐

---

## 🌍 1.1 The World of Cybersecurity

### **1.1.1 What Is Cybersecurity?** 🛡️
- Cybersecurity is the ongoing effort to protect networks, devices, programs, and data from attack, damage, or unauthorized access.
- At a personal and organizational level, it involves protecting all computing resources from theft and damage.

### **1.1.2 Protecting Your Personal Data** 🔒
- Personal information such as ID numbers, birth dates, and passwords are all valuable to attackers.
- Protecting this data requires being cautious about what is shared online and how it is stored.

### **1.1.3 Your Online Identity** 👤
- Your online identity is made up of two parts: your **offline identity** (who you are in real life) and your **online identity** (who you are in the digital space).
- Online identity should only reveal a limited amount of information about you.

### **1.1.4 Your Data** 📂
- Any information about you is considered your data — including medical, education, employment, and financial records.
- This data has real value and is a target for cybercriminals.

### **1.1.5 Where Is Your Data?** 🗄️
- Personal data is stored in many places — medical offices, schools, employers, insurance companies, and businesses you interact with.
- Because it is spread across multiple locations, it becomes harder to fully control or protect.

### **1.1.6 What's More...** ➕
- Beyond personal records, your online activity, browsing habits, and social media interactions also generate data that can be tracked and collected.

### **1.1.7 Smart Devices** 📱⌚
- Everyday smart devices (phones, wearables, home assistants) collect and transmit personal data.
- These devices increase convenience but also increase the number of ways your data can be exposed.

### **1.1.8 What Do Hackers Want?** 🕵️
- Hackers seek data with monetary value — such as banking details, medical records, education records, and employment information.
- This stolen data can be sold or used for further attacks.

### **1.1.9 Identity Theft** 🎭
- Identity theft occurs when someone steals your personal information to impersonate you, often for financial gain.
- It can involve stolen documents, malware, phishing, or Wi-Fi eavesdropping.

### **1.1.10 Who Else Wants My Data?** 🏢
- Beyond hackers, businesses, and even employers may also seek access to personal data for marketing, hiring, or advertising purposes.

## 🏢 1.2 Organizational Data

- **Define:** Organizations store several types of data, including traditional records (personnel, financial, product information) and Internet of Things (IoT) data collected from connected devices.
- **Why it matters:** Organizational data is a high-value target because a single breach can expose the data of many individuals at once.

---

## 💥 1.3 What Was Taken?

- **Define:** Covers real-world examples and consequences of data breaches — what type of information was stolen (financial, health, intellectual property) and the resulting impact.
- **Why it matters:** Understanding real breaches helps illustrate the scale and seriousness of cyberattacks.

---

## 🕶️ 1.4 Cyber Attackers

- **Define:** Introduces different categories of attackers:
  - **Amateur hackers** — often using existing tools with limited skill.
  - **Hackers** — categorized as white hat (ethical) ⚪, gray hat ⚫⚪, and black hat (malicious) ⚫.
  - **Organized hackers** — including cybercriminals, hacktivists, and state-sponsored attackers.
- **Why it matters:** Knowing attacker types helps in understanding motives — financial gain, political statements, or espionage.

---

## ⚔️ 1.5 Cyberwarfare

- **Define:** Cyberwarfare is internet-based conflict involving politically motivated attacks on information systems, often carried out by nation-states.
- **Why it matters:** These attacks can target critical infrastructure (power grids ⚡, military systems 🎖️, financial systems 💰) and have national or global consequences.
